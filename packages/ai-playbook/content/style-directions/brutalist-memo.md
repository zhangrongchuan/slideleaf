## When To Use
- Hard decision deck where clarity matters more than polish.
- Turnaround, restructuring, or strategic reset.
- Internal memo converted into a presentation.

## When Not To Use
- Investor pitch that needs warmth.
- Product launch.
- Customer-facing deck where tone should be collaborative.

## Good Structure
- Use stark typography, clear rules, and strong sections.
- Keep slides direct and low-decoration.
- Let action titles carry the argument.
- Use tables and callouts only when necessary.

## Anti Patterns
- Harsh visuals that feel careless.
- All-caps body copy.
- No evidence behind strong claims.
- Deliberately ugly layout.

## Evidence Requirements
- Strong claims need explicit evidence or assumption labels.

## Suggested Visual Components
- decision-callout
- comparison-table
- risk-matrix
- executive-summary

## Example Markup
```html
<section class="slide style-brutalist-memo" data-motion="static">
  <p class="eyebrow">Decision</p>
  <h1>The company should stop funding broad expansion until retention recovers</h1>
</section>
```

## QA Checklist
- Starkness reads as intentional.
- Evidence supports the direct tone.
- Text hierarchy is clean.
- The slide is not visually sloppy.
