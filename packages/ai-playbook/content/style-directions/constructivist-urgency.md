## When To Use
- Transformation narrative.
- Urgent launch or turnaround deck.
- Strategic call-to-action where momentum is part of the message.

## When Not To Use
- Calm board governance.
- Conservative investor financial appendix.
- Detailed technical diagrams.

## Good Structure
- Use bold diagonals, high contrast, and strong typographic hierarchy.
- Reserve the style for section openers or high-energy narrative slides.
- Pair with quieter analytical slides to avoid fatigue.

## Anti Patterns
- Constant visual shouting.
- Red/black overuse that feels alarming.
- Diagonals that make content hard to read.
- Aggressive style without evidence.

## Evidence Requirements
- Urgency claims must have timing evidence.

## Suggested Visual Components
- section-break
- narrative-timeline
- before-after-flow
- decision-callout

## Example Markup
```html
<section class="slide style-constructivist" data-motion="progressive-reveal">
  <p class="eyebrow">Inflection point</p>
  <h1>The next two quarters decide whether the company captures or follows the category</h1>
</section>
```

## QA Checklist
- Use sparingly.
- Text remains readable.
- Urgency is evidence-backed.
- Adjacent slides provide calmer analytical contrast.
