## When To Use
- User research synthesis.
- Customer discovery evidence.
- Qualitative insight slide that needs authenticity.

## When Not To Use
- Formal board financial update.
- Pure technical architecture.
- When user quotes or artifacts are unavailable.

## Good Structure
- Use evidence snippets, interview tags, and synthesis labels.
- Separate observation from interpretation.
- Pair qualitative evidence with a decision implication.
- Keep texture subtle and readable.

## Anti Patterns
- Fake sticky-note chaos.
- Long quotes.
- Anecdote presented as universal fact.
- Decorative notebook style without research evidence.

## Evidence Requirements
- Interview count or source label.
- Quote, observation, or support-ticket source.
- Clear limitation statement if sample is small.

## Suggested Visual Components
- quote-grid
- insight-stack
- evidence-wall
- user-journey

```html

  <p class="eyebrow">Customer evidence</p>

  <div class="quote-grid"></div>

```

## QA Checklist
- Observation and interpretation are separated.
- Quotes are short.
- Source context is visible.
- Qualitative claim is not overgeneralized.
