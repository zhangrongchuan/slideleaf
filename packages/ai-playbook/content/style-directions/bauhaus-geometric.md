## When To Use
- Product strategy or brand-forward executive deck.
- Category creation narrative needing energy without becoming playful.
- Slides that benefit from simple geometric composition.

## When Not To Use
- Formal board update.
- Highly regulated financial deck.
- Complex data-heavy slides where geometry would compete with evidence.

## Good Structure
- Use simple geometric blocks, strong alignment, and limited primary accent colors.
- Combine large type with clean functional shapes.
- Keep composition asymmetric but organized.

## Anti Patterns
- Poster art without business logic.
- Too many colors.
- Decorative circles or shapes that do not structure content.
- Poor text fit inside geometric forms.

## Evidence Requirements
- Style has no extra evidence requirement, but business claims still need sources.

## QA Checklist
- Shapes create hierarchy.
- Palette stays limited.
- Business message remains dominant.
- Text never clips inside shapes.
