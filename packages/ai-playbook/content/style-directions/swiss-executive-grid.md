## When To Use
- CEO, board, consulting, investor, and strategy decks that need authority.
- Dense analytical slides requiring precise alignment.
- Data-heavy decks where restraint improves credibility.

## When Not To Use
- Playful consumer launch.
- Highly emotional brand campaign.
- Deck that needs immersive imagery as the primary signal.

## Good Structure
- Use a strong grid, left alignment, generous whitespace, and restrained accent color.
- Let typography and data carry authority.
- Favor clear hierarchy over decorative containers.
- Use thin rules, small labels, and precise spacing.

## Anti Patterns
- Decorative cards everywhere.
- Overly centered layouts.
- Soft gradients replacing structure.
- Too many type sizes.

## Evidence Requirements
- None specific to style, but data claims must remain sourced.

## Suggested Visual Components
- executive-summary
- comparison-table
- metric-system
- issue-tree
- 2x2-matrix

## Example Markup
```html
<section class="slide style-swiss" data-motion="static">
  <p class="eyebrow">Decision</p>
  <h1>Concentrating on one wedge improves speed without sacrificing margin quality</h1>
</section>
```

## QA Checklist
- Grid alignment is obvious.
- Accent color has a purpose.
- No decorative blobs, oversized cards, or vague gradients.
- Typography stays readable at presentation distance.
