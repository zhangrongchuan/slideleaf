## When To Use
- Market entry strategy.
- New segment prioritization.
- Strategic growth thesis that must move from diagnosis to recommendation.

## When Not To Use
- Pure fundraising pitch where proof of traction matters more than market diagnosis.
- Product release note.
- Internal design critique.

## Good Structure
- Frame opportunity and constraints.
- Segment the market using decision-relevant criteria.
- Assess attractiveness, competitive coverage, capability fit, and entry risk.
- Recommend wedge, sequencing, and proof milestones.

## Anti Patterns
- TAM-only logic.
- Segment names that do not change the decision.
- Competitor maps with vague axes.
- Recommendation before diagnosis.

## Evidence Requirements
- Segment size and growth.
- Customer need intensity.
- Competitive coverage or whitespace evidence.
- Capability fit and entry barrier assessment.

## QA Checklist
- Segmentation is MECE enough for the decision.
- Whitespace is justified with evidence.
- Recommendation follows the analysis chain.
- The first execution phase is concrete.
